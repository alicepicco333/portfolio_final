About Me

Hello, I'm Alice!

I'm a Digital Humanities Student at University of Bologna and Intern at Institute of Network Cultures, Amsterdam.

I am interested in the intersection between anthropology, the digital humanities and design. My practice is multidisciplinary research on digital culture and some my expertises iclude web development, creative coding, data visualization, UX and interaction design.

Words in gender

→ website

Digital Edition

The final project of the course Digital Text in the Humanities, Words in Gender is a digital edition inspired by Vera Gheno's Parole d'Altro Genere. The integrated visualization allows to visualize a set of snippets of text referring to or containing a keyword related to women, such as body, beauty, motherhood ... from a corpus of selected texts written by woman authors.

cAPPellini

→ project page

UX Design, Interaction Design

cAPPellini is a prototype of a gamification app for children visiting the geology museum Giovanni Capellini in Bologna. It is a mobile application offering a quiz with a guided tour of selected pieces of the collection. First it plays video-animated sequences of a cartoon G. Capellini himsef narrating the selected object in the collection, then it asks the player to answer a quiz question.

Digitalian Folktales

→ website

Digital Edition

Digitalian folktales is an interactive collection of folktales inspired by Italo Calvino's Fiabe Italiane. In our exploration, we discovered a common theme within folktales from Sicily and Friuli-Venezia-Giulia, as documented in Calvino's collection. Captivated by this shared narrative, we obtained the original versions in Sicilian and Friulian, translating them into Italian. Ultimately, we developed a website where the three distinct versions could be juxtaposed and examined.
